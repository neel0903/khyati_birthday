A Confession

something I've been carrying for you

My Khyatu,

I want to start with the most important thing — I am sorry, Khyatu. I am sorry for everything that hurt you because of me. Since the day I entered your life, I know I have given you reasons to feel heavy. The fights we had, the things I said, the things I didn't say — every one of them made your heart heavier when all I wanted was to make it lighter. I think about those moments more than you know. And I just want to say it plainly today — I'm sorry. For every part of me that ever caused you pain, I'm sorry.

When you don't reply, I notice. Not in an angry way — in a quiet, aching way. I check the time. I wonder if I said something wrong. I scroll back through our last messages looking for the moment I might have lost you. And when you do reply, even with one word, my whole day changes. That's not exaggeration. That is exactly how it works inside me.

And on the days when you don't talk to me at all — I'm honestly not okay. I keep busy. I do my work, I eat, I show up for everything I'm supposed to. But somewhere inside, it doesn't feel right. I miss you. I miss your voice. I miss the small messages that pop up and make my whole day feel lighter. I don't think I've figured out how to feel fully like myself without you around. Maybe I never will. Maybe I'm not supposed to.

✿ ✿ ✿

Here is the truth I want you to hold today, on your birthday, the most:

I am bound to you, Khyatu. In every phase of my life, in every version of me — you are there. You are the steady thing. I know sometimes you might not believe how deeply I feel this. Words can sound rehearsed, and I know I'm not always the best at saying them. But this — this is not a script. This is me. These are my real feelings, written by the hand that wants to hold yours.

So today, on your first birthday as my wife, I want you to know — I am right here. I am proud of you. I am grateful for you. I am soft for you in a way I didn't know I could be for anyone.
